I have decided to start writing a blog. Well, no, that is a lie. I decided to write a blog some time ago, and just now I got to work on it. In this first post, I would like to provide some background, outline my motivations, and provide a brief overview of my *current* vision for this page.

tl;dr: I am starting this blog to force myself to finish data science projects and to improve my writing.

***

## Background

For some time, I have been talking myself in and out of writing a blog.

I am a curious person by nature. I really enjoy learning new concepts and tinkering with ideas, as well as going down rabbit holes. As I enter the third decade of existence, I find myself in possession of a large "blob" of loosely categorized Jupyter notebooks, with half-baked code and conclusions.

They are too precious to me to delete, but not polished enough to be useful or presentable; in other words, they are not **finished**. What does it mean to be finished? Can anything be truly, completely, thoroughly finished? *Perfect is the enemy of good*, and all of that.

Possibly influenced by writing research papers during my PhD, a thought emerged: "it is finished when it can be presented publicly". This prompted me to start going through some of my old projects, polishing them, and putting them on a GitHub repository. In doing that I realized that **not having a "deliverable" had been stopping me from wrapping up projects**. And just like that, the deliverables manifested, and I found myself more competently planning what to work on and how to present it as a readable notebook. The very notion of "having to present" the final result changed the way I approached my data science explorations.

It was around that time that I began more seriously considering writing a blog. I had done a fair amount of scientific writing, and I really enjoyed it (not claiming I was good, only that I enjoyed it!); incidentally, some of my notebooks started reading like posts. I then began pondering on technical aspects: where would I host the blog? Should I have a personal website or just a blog? What kind of content would it be worth posting about?

I had been dabbling in self-hosting, and thus I found myself laying awake at night thinking about the dangers of making my home server open to the public, firewalls, Cloudflare tunnels, and so on; in other words **a technical aspect had side-tracked me from my original intention**, which was writing! I was in such an unproductive mindset when I came across Eleventy, a static-site generator. Since I was already using Obsidian extensively for note-taking and personal knowledge management, the idea of directly generating a website from Markdown files and HTML/CSS templates was immediately attractive to me. Lastly, the fact that static sites could be easily hosted in a number of places, including GitHub pages, quickly sealed the deal.

## Motivation

As foreshadowed in the background, I want to use this public-facing page as a way to encourage myself to wrap up side projects. In particular, I want to be more conscious and explicit about my goals and framing, and be more (positively) critical of my output.

I also want to practice and improve my writing. I often come across blog posts with such eloquent, concise, and clear writing, that it almost feels like the author is telepathically transferring their knowledge to me.

Finally, I would like to use this page as an overview of my interests and side-projects, a portfolio of sorts. I have no online presence as of now, and I hope this page serves as an effective presentation.

## The vision

A bit dramatic, maybe.

**Content**: I have a number of computational projects (algorithms, statistics, visualization, machine learning, physics) I would like to write about, and no doubt, new ones will come. Some of them might be short, one-shot explorations, which fit in a single notebook/post, and some may summarize part of a more expansive content which can be found in a separate repository. I may even write some tutorials!

**Format**: The idea right now is to store the code and notebooks for self-contained posts in the very same repository as the blog, and to put links to external repositories when applicable. I would like to have some top-level organization, such as "tutorials", "software", "book notes", and so on. I also would like to have some posts be part of a larger, ordered collection of posts, like a series.

**Outreach**: Given the motivation with which I started this site, exposure is at the bottom of my list of priorities. Because of this, and for the sake of simplicity, I do not intend to incorporate any socially interactive elements to it. On the other hand, I would be very happy if you reach out to me with suggestions, corrections (I would **especially** appreciate this), or complaints. For now, feel free to open an issue on the GitHub repository. If at some point I am more comfortable being associated with this site, I will incorporate some form of contact information.

***

With this, I conclude my first post. Time will tell how well these paragraphs age. In the meanwhile, thank you for taking the time to read me, I will hopefully see you around.

Mikel
